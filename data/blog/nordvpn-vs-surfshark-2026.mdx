# Security Comparison

When evaluating cybersecurity tools, the underlying security infrastructure is the most critical factor. Both NordVPN and Surfshark utilize industry-leading technology, but their specific implementations differ.

## Encryption
Both NordVPN and Surfshark employ AES-256-GCM encryption, which is the current gold standard in the cybersecurity industry. This level of encryption is utilized by government agencies, military organizations, and financial institutions worldwide to protect top-secret data. Brute-forcing AES-256 encryption using modern computing power is considered mathematically impossible, ensuring that your data remains completely confidential while in transit over either network.

## Protocols
The VPN protocol determines how your data is routed and encrypted. Surfshark relies primarily on the open-source WireGuard protocol, which is celebrated for its lightweight codebase, rapid connection times, and excellent efficiency on mobile devices. It also supports older, proven protocols like OpenVPN and IKEv2 as fallbacks.

NordVPN, on the other hand, utilizes NordLynx, a proprietary protocol built around the core architecture of WireGuard. NordVPN engineered NordLynx to eliminate a minor privacy concern inherent to standard WireGuard (which requires storing user IP addresses temporarily on the server). By implementing a custom double NAT (Network Address Translation) system, NordLynx delivers the incredible speeds of WireGuard while ensuring absolute privacy.

## Kill Switch
A kill switch is a mandatory fail-safe. If your connection to the VPN server drops unexpectedly, the kill switch instantly severs your device's internet access, preventing your true IP address from leaking to your ISP. Both NordVPN and Surfshark offer robust, system-level kill switches. During our network interruption tests, both applications responded instantly, ensuring no unencrypted packets escaped the secure tunnel.

## Threat Protection
This is where the two services begin to diverge. Surfshark offers CleanWeb, a highly effective DNS-level ad and malware blocker. It successfully prevents intrusive advertisements, tracking scripts, and known phishing domains from loading on your device, significantly improving page load times and reducing bandwidth consumption.

NordVPN goes a step further with Threat Protection. While it also blocks ads and trackers, Threat Protection acts more like a lightweight antivirus. It scans downloaded files for malware before they can execute on your system, and it works even when you are not actively connected to a VPN server. This makes Threat Protection a substantially more comprehensive security tool, although you should still rely on the [best password managers](/blog/best-password-managers-2026) and consult our [password manager guide](/blog/what-is-a-password-manager) to store your sensitive credentials, whether you prefer options like [Bitwarden](/blog/bitwarden-review-2026) or [1Password](/blog/1password-review-2026).

## Additional Security Features
NordVPN provides specialized servers for high-risk environments. This includes Double VPN (which routes your traffic through two separate servers, encrypting it twice) and Onion Over VPN (which integrates directly with the Tor network for maximum anonymity). Surfshark counters with MultiHop, its own version of a double VPN, and a rotating IP feature that periodically changes your IP address while keeping your connection active, making it significantly harder to track your digital footprint.

### Winner: NordVPN
While both providers offer impenetrable encryption and secure protocols, NordVPN wins the security category due to the advanced capabilities of its Threat Protection feature and its proprietary NordLynx protocol, which offers a slight edge in privacy over standard WireGuard.

Surfshark is headquartered in the Netherlands. While the Netherlands is a member of the Nine Eyes intelligence-sharing alliance, this is largely a theoretical concern. ## Transparency
Both companies operate their entire infrastructure on diskless, RAM-only servers. This means that no data is ever written to a permanent hard drive. Every time a server is restarted or updated, all information is instantly and permanently wiped. This volatile memory architecture ensures that even if a physical server were seized by authorities, no usable data could be extracted.

In 2026, marketing claims regarding privacy are insufficient; independent verification is required. Both providers have demonstrated an exceptional commitment to transparency. # VPN Myths Debunked

The rapid adoption of cybersecurity tools has led to several persistent misconceptions. Let's debunk the most common VPN myths.

### VPN Makes You Anonymous
**False.** A VPN significantly enhances your privacy by encrypting your data and masking your IP address from your ISP and local network eavesdroppers. However, it does not make you completely anonymous. It will not protect you if you willingly log into tracking platforms (like Google or Facebook), and it does not stop browser fingerprinting or tracking cookies. True anonymity requires a complex combination of tools, including secure browsers and strict operational security practices.

### VPN Makes Internet Faster
**Mostly False.** In the vast majority of cases, a VPN will slightly decrease your internet speed due to the overhead of encrypting your data and routing it through an intermediary server. The only exception is if your ISP is actively throttling your bandwidth for specific activities (like torrenting or streaming); in that specific scenario, a VPN can hide the activity from your ISP and restore your normal speeds.

### Free VPNs Are Always Good Enough
**False.** Free VPNs are severely restricted. They typically impose strict data caps, drastically throttle connection speeds, and offer very few server locations, making them useless for streaming or large downloads. More importantly, many free VPNs are actually malicious data-harvesting tools disguised as security software.
