## Key Features

According to Surfshark documentation, the service includes a robust set of features that extend beyond basic connection encryption. These integrated tools provide practical utility for managing your daily digital footprint.

### Unlimited Device Connections

The most significant differentiator for Surfshark is its policy regarding simultaneous device connections. While most competitors restrict users to five or ten devices, Surfshark allows an unlimited number of connections under a single subscription. This makes it an highly cost-effective solution for large households or users who possess multiple smartphones, tablets, laptops, and smart home devices. You can secure your entire digital ecosystem without needing to purchase additional licenses.

### CleanWeb

CleanWeb is Surfshark’s integrated ad and malware blocker. When activated, it operates at the DNS level to prevent intrusive advertisements, tracking scripts, and known malicious domains from loading on your device. This feature not only enhances your security by mitigating phishing risks but also improves browsing speeds and reduces bandwidth consumption by stopping heavy ad elements from downloading.

### MultiHop

For individuals requiring an elevated level of privacy, Surfshark offers MultiHop, commonly known as a double VPN connection. Instead of routing your traffic through a single remote server, MultiHop directs it through two separate servers in different countries. This process encrypts your data twice and masks your IP address behind multiple layers, providing enhanced anonymity for users operating in restrictive or heavily monitored environments.

### Alternative ID

Alternative ID is a relatively new and useful privacy tool introduced by Surfshark. It allows users to generate a completely new digital identity, including a customized name, date of birth, and proxy email address. When you register for online services or newsletters that you do not fully trust, you can use this Alternative ID to keep your real personal information private, thereby reducing the risk of targeted spam and data brokerage profiling.

### Alternative Number

Building upon the concept of Alternative ID, Surfshark also offers an Alternative Number feature (often available as a premium add-on). This provides you with a virtual phone number that you can use for online verifications or account registrations. By utilizing a proxy number, you can protect your primary personal phone number from telemarketers, automated spam calls, and potential security breaches associated with two-factor authentication via SMS.

### Kill Switch

A kill switch is a mandatory security feature, and Surfshark implements it effectively across its applications. If your connection to the VPN server drops unexpectedly due to network instability, the kill switch instantly severs your device's internet access. This critical fail-safe ensures that your true IP address and unencrypted data are never exposed to your local network or internet service provider during a temporary disconnection.

### Split Tunneling

Referred to as "Bypasser" within the Surfshark application, split tunneling provides granular control over your network traffic. This feature allows you to select specific applications or websites that will bypass the encrypted tunnel and connect directly to the internet. Split tunneling is practical when you need to access local network devices, use domestic banking applications that block foreign IP addresses, or optimize speeds for low-risk activities while keeping the rest of your traffic secure.

### WireGuard Support

Surfshark fully supports the WireGuard protocol, which has become the modern standard for secure networking. WireGuard is designed to be significantly faster and more efficient than older protocols like OpenVPN, while still maintaining state-of-the-art cryptography. By utilizing WireGuard, Surfshark delivers rapid connection times, high-speed data transfers, and improved battery life on mobile devices, making it an optimal choice for streaming and large file downloads.

## Security and Privacy

A virtual private network must provide verifiable security measures to be considered reliable. Surfshark utilizes industry-standard technology and transparent policies to protect user data.

### Encryption Standards

Surfshark secures all user traffic using AES-256-GCM encryption. This standard is universally recognized by cybersecurity professionals, government agencies, and financial institutions as virtually impenetrable. Even with access to immense computational power, it is currently considered impossible to decipher AES-256 encryption. By deploying this robust cipher, Surfshark ensures that your data remains completely confidential while in transit over the internet.

### No-Logs Policy

The core of Surfshark's privacy commitment is its strict no-logs policy. The company explicitly states that it does not monitor, track, or retain user activity data. This means that your browsing history, connection timestamps, IP addresses, and downloaded files are never recorded. Because Surfshark does not possess this information, it cannot be handed over to third parties, advertisers, or government authorities, ensuring your online actions remain private.

### Independent Audits

To validate its no-logs claims, Surfshark regularly commissions independent audits from respected cybersecurity firms such as Deloitte. These external audits involve comprehensive examinations of Surfshark's server infrastructure and internal configurations. Independent audits have verified that the company's operational practices align with its privacy policy, demonstrating that the no-logs guarantee is a factual reality rather than a marketing slogan.

### Jurisdiction

Surfshark is headquartered in the Netherlands. While the Netherlands is a member of the Nine Eyes intelligence-sharing alliance, it is important to understand the practical implications. Because Surfshark operates under a verified no-logs policy and utilizes volatile memory servers, there is no historical data available to share. Even if a legal request for user data is issued, Surfshark physically cannot provide information that does not exist on their network.

### RAM-Only Servers

A critical component of Surfshark's security architecture is its deployment of RAM-only servers. Traditional servers store operating systems and data on physical hard drives, which can theoretically be seized and analyzed. Surfshark runs its entire network infrastructure entirely on volatile Random Access Memory (RAM). Consequently, any data passing through the server is instantly and permanently wiped every time the server is restarted or powered down, effectively eliminating the risk of long-term data extraction.

## Limitations of Surfshark

When evaluating any cybersecurity tool, acknowledging its trade-offs is essential for an objective assessment. Based on publicly available information and performance metrics, here are the primary limitations to consider.

### Not Always the Fastest VPN

While Surfshark utilizes the modern WireGuard protocol, speed test results indicate that performance can be variable. Speeds are generally reliable when connecting to local or regional servers; however, significant drops in connection speed can occur when routing traffic through long-distance servers. Performance may vary depending on location and server selection. For users who require the absolute fastest peak speeds for massive file transfers or competitive gaming, competitors like NordVPN or ExpressVPN may outperform Surfshark in standardized tests.

### Renewal Pricing

A common industry practice among VPN providers is offering steep introductory discounts to attract new customers. Surfshark employs this strategy aggressively. While the initial two-year subscription offers a relatively low monthly rate, the renewal costs are typically much higher. Pricing may change over time, and users should verify current subscription costs and carefully read the renewal terms before committing to a long-term plan to avoid unexpected charges.

### Jurisdiction Considerations

Surfshark is headquartered in the Netherlands. The Netherlands is a cooperating member of the Nine Eyes intelligence-sharing alliance, which means the government could legally request data sharing with allied nations. This jurisdiction is not as strictly privacy-focused as Panama (NordVPN) or Switzerland (Proton VPN). However, Surfshark mitigates this concern with its RAM-only server architecture and an independently audited no-logs policy, meaning they technically have no historical data to hand over even if compelled. Still, for extreme privacy purists, the jurisdiction remains a limitation.

### Advanced Users May Prefer Alternatives

Surfshark is designed for broad consumer appeal, focusing on user-friendliness and essential features. Advanced users or network administrators who require specialized privacy tools—such as complex enterprise networking configurations, dedicated port forwarding, or deeply integrated router applications—may find Surfshark lacking. For individuals whose threat models necessitate extreme operational security, specialized alternatives like Proton VPN or enterprise-grade networking solutions are generally more appropriate.
